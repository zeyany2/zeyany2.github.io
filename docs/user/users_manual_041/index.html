Even with its limitations, there are many applications for superelements in nonlinear and buckling analysis. Applying Loads on Upstream Superelements in Nonlinear Statics MSC Nastran offers two nonlinear static solutions: SOL 106 and SOL 400 with ANALYSIS =NLSTATIC.
    Both solutions provide the nonlinear capabilities of large displacement, differential stiffness, follower force stiffness, material nonlinear, and contact. SOL 106 is limited to small strain theory and contact with CGAP and slidelines. SOL 106 is not under active development. SOL 400 offers nonlinear capabilities of large displacement, differential stiffness, follower force stiffness, material nonlinear (including large strain), and contact. SOL 400 contact is general body-to-body contact and offers many ease-of-use capabilities with respect to contact setup and solution. SOL 400 also has advanced convergence schemes and advanced nonlinear formulations.
    The method that superelements are implemented in SOL 106 and SOL 400 is similar as far as List Superelements and PART Superelements with the exception of defining the loading on the upstream superelement. Currently neither of the nonlinear static solutions support external superelements. The following subsections describe the differences in applying loads to the upstream superelements for each solution.
    Superelement Loading in SOL 106 The case control for SOL 106 nonlinear statics is the same as the case control for linear statics with one exception: static loads on upstream superelements cannot be directly referenced by the LOAD case control command.
